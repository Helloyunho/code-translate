## Design Context

### Users
Primary users are developers translating code snippets quickly between programming languages.
Typical context is in-flow development work where speed and clarity matter more than decoration.

### Brand Personality
Personality target: trustworthy, polished, practical.
Reference quality bar aligns with major productivity products (Google, Apple, Microsoft): clean, restrained, and high signal-to-noise.
Emotional goal: confidence and focus.

### Aesthetic Direction
Direction: minimal and professional, with equal emphasis on light and dark modes.
Use calm surfaces, strong readability, and clear hierarchy.
Avoid toy-like visuals and avoid flashy/low-quality "dribbble bait" styling.

### Design Principles
1. Clarity first: prioritize legibility, fast scanning, and obvious actions.
2. Functional minimalism: every visual element must serve usability.
3. Professional restraint: no gimmicks, no noisy decoration, no playful styling.
4. Consistent parity: light and dark themes should feel equally polished.
5. Inclusive defaults: semantic labels and accessible controls for assistive technologies.
